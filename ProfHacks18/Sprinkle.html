Sprinkle

A personalized sprinkler system that uses the precipitation levels and soil moisture of the user’s home to determine where or not to “sprinkle”.

LOG IN

Soil Moisture Sensor

Measuring the current that passes through our two nail wired system determines how moist the soil is. Since water is conductive, a lower current indicates dry soil, then allowing users to sprinkle.

LOG IN

Weather API

The user’s zip code allows the Arduino to access current and future precipitation levels in that area. If there is rainfall during the time the user wants to turn the system on, it will not sprinkle.

OUR STORY


Conserving water reduces the effects of drought, cuts the cost of energy bills and prolongs our access to clean water – a finite resource.


According to the Saving Water Partnership, automatic sprinklers waste “40%-50% of the water used.” By choosing Sprinkle ©, your lawn will receive the care that it needs, your wallet will feel a little heavier, and most importantly, the environment will become greener.

THE DATA

As Oklahoma experiences a drought, Sprinkle’s soil moisture data is able to display which counties are overwatered and which are in need of a sprinkle. With this information, our system can conserve water by evenly distributing sprinkles across the state.
